Our story

Clothes that carry a place with them.

Indus Threads started in 2024 in a one-room studio in Shahpur Jat, Delhi — three design graduates, a screen-printing table, and a shared frustration that "Indian streetwear" usually meant a cliché slogan tee. We wanted something that felt closer to home: the patterns on a grandmother's saree, the jaali screens of old Delhi havelis, the block prints drying on rooftops in Bagru.

Why we exist

Roots, not references

Most "ethnic streetwear" treats Indian motifs as a backdrop. We start from the motif itself — its geometry, its history, the craft behind it — and build a garment around that, not the other way round.

How we make it

Small batches, real workshops

Every print run is produced in limited batches with print and stitching units across Delhi-NCR. Smaller runs mean less waste, tighter quality control, and fairer pay per piece.

How we got here

From sketchbook to street

A short timeline of how a college project became a label.

2023

The first print

A final-year textile project reimagining Ajrakh patterns for graphic tees — shown at a college exhibition in Delhi.

2024

Studio opens in Shahpur Jat

Indus Threads is founded with a small screen-printing setup and a first run of 50 tees, sold through Instagram.

2025

First hoodie drop

Partnered with a fleece manufacturer in Faridabad to launch the Ajrakh Block Hoodie — sold out in 48 hours.

2026

The Jaali Drop

Our most ambitious capsule yet — four pieces built around the geometry of Delhi's sandstone window screens.

What we stand for

The fine print, made plain

Fair pay

Print and stitching partners are paid above standard piece rates, with on-time payments — every batch.

Honest fabric

100% cotton tees and heavyweight cotton-fleece hoodies. No blends marketed as "premium."

Credit where due

Every product page names the print technique and region it draws from — never just "ethnic print."

Made to last

Tested for wash and wear so the print outlasts the trend cycle, not just the first wash.
